<p>&lt;h1&gt;Spatiotemporal Modelling&lt;a class=”headerlink” href=”#spatiotemporal-modelling” title=”Permalink to this headline”&gt;¶&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/h1&gt;
&lt;div class=”section” id=”introduction”&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Introduction&lt;a class=”headerlink” href=”#introduction” title=”Permalink to this headline”&gt;¶&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Suppose&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[{X_i,y_i}_i]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;as our features and response (number of positive influenza cases). We
will assume the following underlying relationship:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[y_i = f(x_i) + epsilon_i,]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;where :math:: &lt;a href=”#id1”&gt;&lt;span class=”problematic” id=”id2”&gt;`x_iinmathbb{R}^p`_&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;is the feature,&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}epsilon_isim N(0,sigma^2)\andend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}f\is the underlying function. Sinceend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}x_i\contains spatial and temporal features, the standard regression methodsend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;of generalised additive models (GAMs), gradient boosting and regression
trees are not adapted to this problem. In addition, pure time series
models such as Long-short term memory (LSTM) recurrent neural networks,
SARIMA and ARMA-GARCH models also do not take in account of the spatial
variation.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;On the other hand, stochastic processes such as Gaussian processes (GP)
or solutions to stochastic partial differential equations (SPDE) are
well-adapted to what we would like to accomplish.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;SPDEs are the most natural approach to modelling spatiotemporal
phenomenon, by adding a driven white noise to a partial differential
equation (PDE) to obtain&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}  Lu = f + xicirc dW,\whereend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}L\is a differential operator,end{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}f\is a function andend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}xicirc dW\is a driven white noise. However, there is limited software to provideend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;solutions to these SPDEs. &lt;code class=”docutils literal notranslate”&gt;&lt;span class=”pre”&gt;R-INLA&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/code&gt; is a library that uses the Bayesian
method integrated nested Laplace approximation (INLA) to construct weak
solutions to linear fractional SPDEs, but this places too much
restriction of the underlying SPDE and would result in blackbox
modelling. ## A Spatiotemporal Model&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In this study, we will thus use GPs as a spatiotemporal framework to
study spatiotemporal variations. We let&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[f]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;have a Gaussian process prior, giving&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}  fsim GP(mu(cdot), k(cdot,cdot)),\whereend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}mu(cdot)\andend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[ begin{align}begin{aligned}k(cdot,cdot)\are chosen mean and covariance functions. We also callend{aligned}end{align} ]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;span class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;(k(cdot,cdot))&lt;/span&gt; a kernel by convention.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The choice of the mean is usually zero mean, constant mean, polynomial
or splines. This helps us capture the trend of&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;div class=”math notranslate nohighlight”&gt;
[y_i]&lt;/div&gt;
&lt;p&gt;.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;However, perhaps what would be more important is capturing the
covariance between different features.&lt;/p&gt;
